Spirited Away

Director: Hayao Miyazaki
Cast:
  • Rumi Hiiragi as Chihiro
  • Miyu Irino as Haku
  • Mari Natsuki as Yubaba / Zeniba
  • Takashi Naitô as Akio Ogino
  • Yasuko Sawaguchi as Yûko Ogino
  • Bunta Sugawara as Kamaji
Release Date: July 20, 2001

Synopsis

Spirited Away is an acclaimed animated masterpiece directed by Hayao Miyazaki, which follows the journey of a 10-year-old girl named Chihiro as she discovers a mysterious world inhabited by spirits and supernatural beings. The adventure begins when Chihiro and her parents stumble upon an abandoned amusement park, only to realize it serves as a gateway to the spirit realm. After her parents are transformed into pigs by a witch named Yubaba, Chihiro must navigate this enchanting yet perilous world to save them and return to the human realm. With the help of a mysterious boy named Haku and other unique characters, Chihiro faces challenges that test her courage and determination while uncovering her own strength. The film explores themes of identity, resilience, and the balance between humanity and nature. Released on July 20, 2001, Spirited Away continues to be celebrated as a monumental work in animation, earning widespread acclaim for its storytelling, artistry, and emotional depth.

Ponyo

Director: Hayao Miyazaki
Cast:
  • Yuria Nara as Ponyo
  • Hiroki Doi as Sōsuke
  • Tomoko Yamaguchi as Lisa
  • Kazushige Nagashima as Kōichi
  • Yūki Amami as Granmamare
  • George Tokoro as Fujimoto
Release Date: July 19, 2008

Synopsis

Ponyo is a heartwarming animated film directed by Hayao Miyazaki, which tells the enchanting tale of a young goldfish named Ponyo who dreams of becoming human. After escaping from her father's oceanic home, Ponyo befriends a five-year-old boy named Sōsuke, who finds her stranded on the shore. Their bond grows as Ponyo's desire to live on land strengthens, leading her to use magical powers to transform herself into a human girl. However, her actions disrupt the natural balance of the world, causing the seas to rise and threaten the town.

As Ponyo and Sōsuke embark on a journey to restore harmony, they face challenges that test their courage and friendship. This visually stunning and emotionally rich story explores themes of love, trust, and the delicate connection between humanity and nature. It was released on July 19, 2008, and has since become a beloved classic in Studio Ghibli's collection.
