South Korea

The Vibrant Culture of South Korea

Why South Korea?

    South Korea is one of the countries I deeply wish to explore. The blend of its traditional heritage and modern innovation fascinates me, but what captivates me most is its culture. My love for K-pop is a huge motivator behind this dream, as it’s not just a genre of music but a whole cultural phenomenon that resonates with me. Visiting the buildings of my ultimate (ult) groups and seeing where some of their creative magic happens would be an unforgettable experience.

    Beyond that, I also want to soak in the beauty of South Korea’s popular places, from vibrant markets like Myeongdong to serene spots such as Jeju Island or the ancient palaces of Seoul. The food culture, bustling city life, and natural beauty make it a destination that offers both adventure and personal connection to something I cherish. Strolling through traditional hanok villages or enjoying the modernity of Gangnam would be equally inspiring, immersing me in the vibrant and diverse beauty of South Korea.

How to go there?

Fly into Incheon International Airport, the main gateway to South Korea. From there, the country’s extensive public transportation system makes it easy to reach various destinations, including trains, subways, and buses.

Other places to visit in South Korea

Bukchon Hanok Village, Seoul

Step back in time as you wander through this beautifully preserved village showcasing traditional Korean architecture.

Jeju Island

Explore South Korea's crown jewel of nature, offering stunning beaches, volcanic landscapes, and lush hiking trails.

Haeundae Beach, Busan

Relax on Busan's most popular beach and soak in the vibrant energy of its bustling promenade and lively atmosphere.
